Green and orange politics

Social stuff:

  • John Howard: "The Greens are not just about the environment. They have a whole lot of other very, very kooky policies in relation to things like drugs and all of that sort of stuff"
  • The "kooky" policies are the main reason I support them; much higher level of agreement wrt. views on asylum seekers, respect for minority rights, legalisation of drugs, voluntary euthanasia, etc than major parties
  • Those are all problems pretty amenable to social science and hard evidence
  • Less certain about economic policies: harder to consider in isolation than policies concerning individual people, systems are complicated
  • Australia is rich enough that economic concerns aren't really the main ones right now anyway?

Environmentalism:

  • My support for conservation is strongly tied to the threat climate change poses to human lives
  • It's beautiful, like artwork, but how much do I care about art except insofar as people are affected by it?
  • Suffering of animals-- more complicated, especially considering whales and elephants. Still haven't figured out how to think about this one
  • "unless it can be proven not to hurt" - sometimes the risks inherent to continuing to exist in our current state outweigh the risk of new technology: lots of threats which we currently don't have the capability to stop
  • Humans are a part of nature, and can "construct nature": royal botanic gardens most beautiful place I've been, but undeniably artificial. False dichotomy between technology and nature.
  • Would be pretty happy to see the entire planet covered in parks and sustainable cities even if it meant no true wilderness remained?
  • Hydroelectricity very effective renewable energy source, but greens often oppose construction of dams
  • Occasional green focus on localism and preservation of ancient culture may conflict with orange focus on global interconnectedness and empathy
